Detailed Event Rules

1. Civil Digi-Expo (Digital Poster Presentation)

Description: An art of communication required for every engineer in delivering technical knowledge.

  • Eligibility: Limited to Civil Engineering students only.
  • Team Size: Maximum of two participants.
  • Submission: Digital Poster must be presented in a single PDF or image format.
  • Presentation: Description related to the Digital Poster is to be described at the time of presentation.
  • Themes: Trends in Concrete Technology, Green Building Technology, Watershed management, Application of Geosynthetics, Intelligent Transport Systems, Applications of GIS, Low Cost Housing Technologies.

2. KRITHI QUEST (Technical Quiz)

Description: Exciting event to explore civil engineering concepts with questions from the civil engineering domain.

  • Team Size: A team consists of two members (B.Tech., Civil Engineering Students).
  • Format: Preliminary test will be conducted to select **SIX TEAMS** for the final quiz event.
  • Final Rounds: Team Question Round, Picture Question Round, Rapid Fire Round.

3. Samsodhana (Technical Paper Presentation)

Description: Aimed to encourage students to review latest literature, prepare a technical report, and improve communication skills.

  • Eligibility: Open to all civil engineering students with novel ideas.
  • Authors: Each paper can have a maximum of **TWO** authors.
  • Format: Papers should be submitted in **MS Word document** (Times New Roman, 12pt font).
  • Length: Restricted to **six A4 size papers**.
  • Submission: Soft copy must be submitted through the Google form link.
  • Deadline: On or before **18-12-2025**.
  • Intimation: Regarding selection will be sent to authors email on or before **22-12-2025**.

4. CADCon (AutoCAD Design Competition)

Description: Challenges participants to showcase creativity, accuracy, and command over AutoCAD software.

  • Eligibility: Limited to **individual** Civil Engineering student only.
  • Shortlisting: Screening test may be conducted.
  • Task: Participants are required to draw a plan for the given problem statement which will be given **on the spot**.
  • Time Limit: Participants will be given **90 minutes** to complete the drawing.
  • Restrictions: Use of mobile phones, internet, and external storage devices during the event is not allowed.

5. TECH HUNT (Technical Treasure Hunt)

Description: Treasure hunt style event designed to test knowledge, problem solving skills, and team work in Civil Engineering.

  • Eligibility: All participants must belong to the Civil Engineering department.
  • Team Size: Maximum **two** members per team.
  • Exclusion: **The students from our college are NOT allowed to participate in this event.**
  • Basis: It will be completely based on technical knowledge.
  • Winning: The team who completes the task first will be selected as the winning team.

6. Aakrithik (Structural Model Construction)

Description: Tests participants' ability to translate theoretical concepts into a stable, load-bearing physical structure (Bridge Model).

  • Team Size: Teams must consist of **2 to 4 members**. Pre-registration is mandatory.
  • Materials: Primary material (Balsa wood sticks, ice-cream sticks, etc.) will be specified/provided. Glue is permitted.
  • Dimensions: Must span a clear distance (e.g., $500 \text{ mm}$ to $1000 \text{ mm}$) and adhere to maximum width/height limits (e.g., $150 \text{ mm}$ wide).
  • Construction: Must be constructed at the venue within a strict time limit (typically 2-4 hours). Pre-fabricated parts are generally not allowed.
  • Evaluation (60%): Based on **Efficiency** (Maximum Load Sustained / Self-Weight of the Model).
